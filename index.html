Alan Perry

Scrolling in the Walled Garden is a web-based meditation on the impact of the smartphone on socioeconomics and data surveillance. Created in the Unity game engine, it is a continually-running simulation of a figure wearing a metallic motion-capture suit walking between different crowds of smaller, 2-dimensional figures using smartphones and working fields.

The visual aesthetics of the artwork reference digital communication technologies, like FaceTime, as well as late medieval altarpieces and illuminated manuscripts. In the center panel is a view of a digital space regularly interrupted by text, while to the left and right are other views of the same landscape. Continuous motion is used to create a visual experience where the viewerâ€™s attention always has shifting demands. Above the landscape are floating stanzas from a poem about engagement with smartphones; the text is difficult to comprehend in an echo of how online reading is typically quick and shallow, with little chance of comprehension, unless done with intentional effort.

Two-dimensional game assets were remixed from historical illuminated manuscripts and content created in collaborator with Ksenia Cherniavksa, a calligrapher and illuminated manuscript painter. Three-dimensional assets were modeled in Autodesk Maya and purchased or downloaded from online game asset marketplaces.
